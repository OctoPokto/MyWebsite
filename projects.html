Mørkredd

Mørkredd is a game developed by Hyper Games AS here in Norway. During my last semester at school I got to work on with the wonderfull team at Hyper Games while I was writing my bachelors thesis. I mostly worked on developing and testing what kind of shaders and effects would enhance the experience for the player.


As this was my first experience working in a team whose goal was to release a game and was experienced doing exactly that, made it a pretty exiting experience. Going from a school mentality where you have multiple projects going at the same time and no real say in when they should be done. To an office setting where I worked on mostly one aspect was very refreshing. Exploring shaders was also a wery rewarding experience as a person who loves making things look pretty/interesting to look at.

You can buy the game on Steam

Robotany

Robotany is a project i worked on at school with three other classmates for two months. The idea is that you play as a robot taking care of plants. Nurturing them with water so they stay healthy and give you recources. The idea came to me when I saw a prototype of a spider-like robot with a potted plant on its head. The game was entered into NGA (Norwegian Game Awards) where it didn't win anything, but one of the judges was very impressed and ranted a bit about how much time he spent on making small gardens which I count as a win.


I wanted to make a cute game where your little robot buddy would carry plants on it's head and plant them where you directed them to. The direction of the concept was admittedly a bit rushed beyond that first idea and gameplay elements were added without much thought due to the time crunch. I really enjoy the concept of blending technology with nature however. I think this is an aspect that is worth taking inot new projects.

Download it on Itch.io. Be warned, it's a bit of a buggy mess.

Cyberpunky

This project was meant as an exploration of level design in the unreal engine. Creating an atmosphere, leading the player towards points of interest and all that. I was the lead designer for the project, directing the visuals, level design and documentation. The name was supposed to be temporary, but nobody really felt like changing it. Thus the very unimaginative name.


I had done a few projects in unreal engine with the goal of learning about game design, but in this project we were more free to create soemthing from scratch. I obviously took inspiration from Cyberpunk 2077 as it was still unreleased and I was excited. The idea is that you are fumbling through the city looking for an access point to cyberspace. You find it in the backroom of a club and enter. Pretty simple, but we had a deadline and several other projects at the same time.

Gamejams

Gamejams are pretty stressfull, but oh so rewarding when you find that weird or simple yet elegant idea and follow it. I've joined a few gamejams over the years with Global Game Jam being the one I usually join. I'm showing the two I'm proud of here as they were pretty fun to work on. The first slide is a game I dubbed "Tower Trouble" where you climb a tower solving small puzzles at each step. I did the design and programming for this one. The second is a game called "A slice of home" where you play as a character calling their parent after leaving their childhood home and living by themselves. I directed a bit, but mostly enjoyed making the music.
